About Gravatar

Gravatar is a web service that allows websites to show little images that represent their users. These images are either user submitted or computer generated.

How Gravatar works

To load an image for a user you take their email address and convert it into an MD5 hash:

  email: joe@example.com
    md5: f5b8fb60c6116331da07c65b96a8a1d1

Gravatar privacy issues

Although it is not possible to turn an md5 back into an email address there are two privacy leaks:

Confirm guessed email address: You can use the md5 in a Gravatar image to confirm if it belongs to an email address.

Link accounts across sites: If the Gravatar md5 for two accounts on different sites are the same they belong to the same email address. This allows you to find all the accounts that an email has on sites that use Gravatar images.
